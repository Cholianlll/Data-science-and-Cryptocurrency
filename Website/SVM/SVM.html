Traditionally, SVMs do not perform well on highly skewed/imbalanced data sets. These are training data sets in which the number of samples that fall in one of the classes far outnumbers those that are a member of the other class.


Another deficiency is that SVMs are not efficient if the number of features is very huge in number compared to the training samples. As the above code shows, the time of tuning parameters is pretty long even it is based on a small range of grid search CV.


However, due to the highly convincing mathematical support behind the SVM, it could achieve an ideal accuracy once the conditions are set.
